I had a Linktree forwarding my domain. I loved it! It was easy, straightforward, and exactly what I was looking for for a professional online presence. But it was limited, and relying on an external platform felt… limiting.  

Then I discovered Github Pages and thought: *Why not just make my own?* So I did.

---

### **The Goal**

* Minimal and clean  
* Central hub for my projects  
* Fast to update  

---

### **Why Linktree-style**

It checks all the boxes. The layout is familiar to anyone on a phone, simple to navigate, and lets me add content at my own pace.

---

### **Layout decisions**

I wanted a centered container with a max-width so it looked good on both mobile and desktop.  

First iteration: my logo, a row of social icons, and a couple of links to external dataset resources. Not much, but the architecture was there.  

Then I added a short bio and a list of projects — content! But it looked smashed together. No visual hierarchy, just white text in a dark container.  

Solution: organize sections in card-style grouping, adjust spacing and padding, and refine the CSS. Suddenly it looked polished and coherent. (Bonus: lots of CSS practice.)

---

### **Current state**

* Clean layout  
* Reusable structure  
* Easy to extend with widgets  

---

### **Tools Used & Skills Sharpened**

* Github Pages  
* Markdown  
* Jekyll  
* CSS  
* HTML  
* ChatGPT  

All on my trusty 2015 MacBook.

---

### **Where it’s going**

I’ll keep tinkering with the site — adjusting CSS, fixing what I break, and expanding content. The landing page will remain the Linktree-style hub, linking to blog posts, project pages, and other experiments.
